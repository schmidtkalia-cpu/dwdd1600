What Is the Solar System?

Our Solar System is a family of worlds orbiting the Sun. It includes eight planets, dwarf planets, moons, asteroids, and comets. Gravity is the glue that keeps everything in motion together.

The inner worlds are rocky, the outer giants are mostly gas or ice. Space seems empty, but there is a bustling neighborhood out there—with dust, debris, and even tiny grains that streak across the sky as meteors.

Quick Highlights

  • The Sun contains over 99% of the Solar System’s mass.
  • Earth is the only known world with life—so far.
  • Spacecraft like Voyager keep traveling beyond the planets.

Key Space Terms (Definition List)

Asteroid
Small rocky object, mostly found in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter.
Comet
Icy body that releases gas and dust when warmed, forming a glowing coma and tail.
Dwarf Planet
Round object orbiting the Sun that has not cleared its orbital neighborhood (e.g., Pluto).
