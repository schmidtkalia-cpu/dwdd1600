Planets (From the Sun Outward)

  1. Mercury — Tiny world with extreme temperature swings.
  2. Venus — Thick atmosphere; hottest surface among the planets.
  3. Earth — Oceans, weather, and life as we know it.
  4. Mars — Dusty deserts, polar caps, and the largest volcano known.
  5. Jupiter — Colossal planet with the Great Red Spot storm.
  6. Saturn — Dazzling rings of ice and rock.
  7. Uranus — Icy giant tipped dramatically on its side.
  8. Neptune — Deep blue world with supersonic winds.

Beyond Neptune lies the Kuiper Belt, a region of icy bodies including Pluto and Eris. Farther still is the Oort Cloud—possibly a vast, distant shell of comets.

Remember: every planet has unique moons and mysteries. Missions continue to reveal surprising details we never expected—space is full of delightful puzzles.

Observation Tips

  • Use a simple star chart app to find planets tonight.
  • Look near sunrise or sunset for bright planets along the ecliptic.
  • Even binoculars can reveal Jupiter’s moons!
