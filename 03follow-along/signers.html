Who Were the Signers

Most Famous Signers

John Hancock
Best known for his large, flamboyant signature at the top of the Declaration. A wealthy Boston merchant and president of the Continental Congress; in the U.S., “John Hancock” became a synonym for “signature.”
Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration; later the 3rd President of the United States and founder of the University of Virginia. His authorship cemented his central place in American identity.
Benjamin Franklin
World-famous scientist, printer, and diplomat by 1776; the oldest signer at 70, renowned for wit and wisdom. His presence added legitimacy and international respect.
John Adams
Leading advocate for independence in Congress; later the 2nd President of the United States. His defense of the Declaration and early role in U.S. politics kept him prominent.
Samuel Adams
Influential revolutionary organizer (e.g., Boston Tea Party, Committees of Correspondence). His fame stemmed more from early resistance leadership than from the Declaration itself.
